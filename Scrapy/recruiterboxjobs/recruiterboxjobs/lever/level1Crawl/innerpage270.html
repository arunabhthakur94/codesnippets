Product Ops: Taking customer support to the next level

Charmaine Ng
Follow
Apr 13, 2016 · 5 min read

What do you do at Lever?

I work on Product Operations, a team of 4 that supports the entire Lever user base! I’ve done tech support at 3 different companies, but none of them compare to life at Lever. I joined because my team is empowered to do more than answer endless tickets and post help articles. We conduct user research, QA features before they ship, and work closely with Customer Success and Sales to keep our customers happy. I also manage our JIRA board and prioritize bugs for engineers.

I’m a writer at heart, but I absolutely love solving problems, investigating bugs, and creating processes. Compared to any other job I’ve had, I really get to leverage my strengths in my role at Lever.

Why pursue support as a career path?

As many do, I fell into support after graduating college, and quickly learned that every day on the job can be different and full of learning. A lot of people assume working in support sucks — they think it’s angry people yelling at you constantly, not getting paid well, and being stuck in monotonous work until burnout. I don’t deny some support jobs are like that, but I’m lucky to be at a company that truly believes in the power of great support as a competitive strategy and values its support employees!

Yes, we still answer a lot of emails, but each interaction with a customer poses a different challenge to solve. A lot of people look for help in accomplishing their hiring goals, optimizing/customizing the product, and so on. As opposed to learning the product just once, each email pushes my team to think about Lever from different points of view and discover new use cases every day.

What sets Product Ops at Lever apart from similar functions at other companies?

Collaboration and investment in internal tools, no doubt. I love putting my investigative hat on and troubleshooting bug reports from customers. The tools allow me to dig in and search for the right answer to help somebody. And if it turns out to be a bug, I can gather basic information and save engineers time.

The engineers are also really passionate about teaching Product Ops more technical skills. Jyrki is especially great at taking the time to investigate issues with us, pulling up queries and showing me how I can do it myself next time. Réal, Jon, Zeus, Emily, and so many of our engineers are talented teachers and genuinely love spreading technical knowledge! I’ve never before worked for a company where engineers care so much about empowering the support team.

Besides that, user needs are taken so seriously, with every bug report, feature request, and feedback. I’ve worked at places that *claimed* to care about users, but it was mostly lip service. When I raised issues to PMs, they would brush me off, indicating they knew better than the customers. I understand that listening to investors and prioritizing sales-requested features are important to growing the business, but Lever maintains the healthiest balance I’ve ever seen.

What’s the best part about working with customers?

It’s easy to take the relationships with users for granted because they build so gradually. There are many individuals I think of fondly — I get excited whenever Mark from MachineZone writes in because he’s so cheerful and helpful. He also understands that bugs often take time to resolve. Brian from Change.org always has thoughtful feedback, which comes from his depth of experience. Our users are incredibly smart and care a lot about recruiting, and they’re so creative and ambitious in wanting to optimize their tool to meet them where they are — I love learning from them.

And there are users who prefer hands-on, high-touch help. Tanya from my team recently answered a string of tickets from a new user over the course of several weeks. The user came back later and mentioned how much she loves working with Tanya and how she feels so supported. I’m so proud of my team for being known for patient and kind over email — no small feat!

Tell us more about the rest of the Product Ops team!

So there’s myself, Tanya, Todd, and Hannah. Todd is a great writer — I’ve learned a lot from him since he joined in January. He asks a lot of probing and thoughtful questions that allow the users to open up and give insightful feedback. He’s figured out how to be a great listener over email, which is pretty amazing!

Hannah is super empathetic to recruiters — you’d think she was a recruiter in a past life. I can get carried away in the investigation and prioritization of bugs, and it’s easy to lose sight of the face on the other side of the computer. Through her actions and thoughtfulness, Hannah reminds me to keep the needs of our users in mind and creatively solve for bugs that affect their experience.

What characteristics do you think set someone up for success in Product Ops?

“Attention to detail” is a cliché, but this team is *very* thorough and inquisitive. We aren’t afraid of going deep to get the job done, while striving for balance and efficiency to keep up with the volume. Understanding how to work with different types of people is also important since we work so closely with engineers and Customer Success to problem-solve. On the flip side, independence and self-sufficiency will serve you well, since resources are always limited in a startup. We also look for “product people” — those who question why things are the way they are and want to understand the people, behavior, and pain points behind the Zendesk tickets.

If any of this resonates, let’s connect! I love meeting other people passionate about support, and my team is hiring. Apply and let’s get the conversation going — the tickets won’t answer themselves :)
